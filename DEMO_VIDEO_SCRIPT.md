# RunFormance Demo Video Script

Target length: 2 minutes 20 seconds. Maximum permitted length: under 3 minutes.

## 0:00–0:15 — The problem

**Visual:** RunFormance Today screen.

**Narration:** “Runners generate useful data across Apple Health, Garmin, and Strava, but the data still leaves one question unanswered: what should I do today? RunFormance turns those fragmented signals into one adaptive, explainable decision.”

## 0:15–0:45 — Daily decision

**Visual:** Point out readiness, workout, best window, AQI, and recovery.

**Narration:** “Today, recovery supports a progression run. RunFormance explains why, estimates the training load, and identifies the best outdoor window using temperature, wind, humidity, and air quality. If smoke or heat worsens, it can shift the time, reduce intensity, or recommend an indoor alternative.”

## 0:45–1:15 — Re-evaluate with GPT-5.6

**Visual:** In AI Adaptive Recommendation, lower sleep and readiness or raise AQI, then select Re-evaluate with GPT-5.6. Show the returned decision, changed inputs, and three reasons.

**Narration:** “For Build Week, RunFormance now uses the OpenAI Responses API with GPT-5.6 as a real adaptive decision engine. Change the runner’s recovery or outdoor conditions, and GPT-5.6 returns a validated keep, modify, delay, or recover decision—then shows exactly which inputs changed and why they matter. The guidance is educational, not medical advice.”

## 1:15–1:35 — Race planning

**Visual:** Open Plan; change race distance and days per week.

**Narration:** “Plans adapt to race distance, date, goals, availability, and recovery. Running, strength, mobility, cycling, and rest work together rather than competing for space.”

## 1:35–1:55 — Explainable coach

**Visual:** Open Coach; select ‘Swap today’s workout,’ submit, show response.

**Narration:** “The coach can explain readiness or preserve the intended benefit when life requires a substitution. Here, it swaps in Zone 2 cycling and moves the quality run to a better recovery and air-quality window.”

## 1:55–2:10 — Privacy and connections

**Visual:** Open Connections; show sources and location controls.

**Narration:** “Users choose which services to connect. Approximate location and rapid-change alerts are optional, and precise routes are never required.”

## 2:10–2:30 — Codex and close

**Visual:** Cycle through the three themes, then return to Today.

**Narration:** “I built RunFormance with Codex and GPT-5.6—from product structure and three visual systems to responsive implementation, interactions, debugging, deployment, and submission preparation. RunFormance: better runs, better recovery, better health.”

## Recording checklist

- Record at 1080p in a desktop browser
- Use a clean browser window and hide bookmarks/personal tabs
- Keep cursor movement deliberate and readable
- Add captions
- Do not imply the sample integrations are already production-authorized
- Upload publicly to YouTube and confirm it plays without signing in
